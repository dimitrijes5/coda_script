Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How can Newport residents easily reach Complete Chiropractic?

We are just 15 km south of Newport, a 20-minute drive via Pittwater Road. The 199 bus also runs directly from Newport to Dee Why, stopping near our clinic.

2. Does cracking my own neck or back provide the same benefits as a chiropractic adjustment?

No, self-cracking may release joint pressure temporarily, but it doesn’t correct spinal misalignments. Chiropractors use precise techniques to adjust specific vertebrae safely. Habitual self-cracking can cause joint instability over time. If you feel the urge to crack your neck or back frequently, consult a chiropractor to address the underlying issue properly.

3. Can I see a chiropractor if I have scoliosis?

Yes, chiropractors can help manage scoliosis symptoms by improving spinal alignment, mobility, and muscle balance. While chiropractic care doesn’t cure scoliosis, it can reduce discomfort, improve posture, and enhance movement. Chiropractors also provide exercises to strengthen core muscles and support spinal stability.

4.  Is it normal to feel sore after a chiropractic adjustment?

Yes, mild soreness after an adjustment is common, especially if it’s your first session or if your muscles are tight. The soreness is similar to post-exercise discomfort and usually resolves within 24–48 hours. Drinking water, stretching, and light movement can help ease any temporary discomfort.

5. How long does chiropractic treatment take to work?

Some patients feel relief immediately, while others notice gradual improvements over several sessions. Acute conditions may improve in a few visits, while chronic issues require longer treatment plans. Chiropractors tailor care to individual needs, adjusting frequency based on progress.
